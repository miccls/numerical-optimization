\subsubsection{Exercise 3.2}
% Use graphical argument and intuition around the conditions.

The Wolfe conditions are the following two inequalities
\begin{align*}
    f(x_k + \alpha p_k)              & \leq f(x_k) + \alpha c_1 \nabla f(x_k)^T p_k \quad \textit{(Sufficient decrease condition)} \\
    \nabla f(x_k + \alpha p_k)^T p_k & \geq c_2 \nabla f(x_k)^T p_k  \quad \textit{(Curvature condition)}
\end{align*}
for $0 < c_1 < c_2 1$.
In this exercise we are to show that if $c_2 < c_1$ there are situations where no step length $\alpha$ satisfies both conditions.

The first condition essentially says that the average slope between $0$ and $\alpha$ should be at most $c_1 \phi(0)$ while the second condition says that the slope at $\alpha$ should be a least $c_2 \phi'(0)$ ($c_2 \times$ the slope at $\phi(0)$).
If $c_2 < c_1$ we can come up with situations where this cannot hold.

Consider a differentiable convex function,\footnote{$f\in C^1(\mathbb{R}) \cap \operatorname{Conv}(\mathbb{R})$ would be the most common set-theoretic notation found in literature on mathematical programming.}
Such functions by the property that
\[
    f(x + \alpha) \geq f(x) + \alpha f'(x).
\]
In words, \textit{a differentiable function of one variable is convex if its graph lies above all of it's tangents.}

If we assume $p_k$ is a descent direction (i.e. $\phi'(0) < 0$), we have
\begin{align*}
    \phi(\alpha)                          & \leq \phi(0) + \alpha c_1 \phi'(0) \\
    \frac{\phi(\alpha) - \phi(0)}{\alpha} & \leq c_1 \phi'(0)
\end{align*}
By the mean value theorem, there exists a $\xi \in (0, \alpha)$ for which $\phi'(\xi)$ is equal to the LHS (average slope).
\begin{align}
    \phi'(\xi) \leq c_1 \phi'(0) \nonumber \\
    \frac{\phi'(\xi)}{\phi'(0)} \geq c_1
    \label{eq:condition_on_c1}
\end{align}

Since $f$, and thus $\phi$, is convex and $\xi < \alpha$,
\[
    c_1 \leq \frac{\phi'(\xi)}{\phi'(0)} \le \frac{\phi'(\alpha)}{\phi'(0)}
\]

Now, the curvature condition is the following
\begin{equation}
    \phi'(\alpha) \geq c_2\phi'(0) \implies \frac{\phi'(\alpha)}{\phi'(0)} \leq c_2
    \label{eq:condition_on_c2}
\end{equation}

Combining \eqref{eq:condition_on_c1}, \eqref{eq:condition_on_c2} we get
\[
    c_1 \leq c_2
\]
which is in direct contradiction with $c_2 < c_1$. That is, for convex functions, there are no valid step lengths satisfying the Wolfe conditions if $c_2 < c_1$.
